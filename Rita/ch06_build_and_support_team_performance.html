Process Group Model for Resources üîÑ

The Resource Management Process groups map most closely to these People Domain skills:

  • Planning: Plan Resource Management, Estimate Activity Resources.
  • Executing: Acquire Resources, Develop Team, Manage Team.
  • Monitoring/Controlling: Control Resources (Note: This is dedicated to material resources like equipment, not team management).

2. PM Leadership Responsibilities üëë

The project manager's role requires formal documentation and continuous planning.

  • Documentation & Formal Processes: Resource management activities are formal and require documentation. The processes of resource management are repeated and updated throughout the project.
  • Roles & Responsibilities: Must be clear.
  • Team Planning: Projects are planned by the team and coordinated by the PM. PM formally plans team building activities in advance‚Äîthese are a required part of project management.
  • Recognition: Creating a recognition and reward system is an important and required resource management function.
  • Competencies: PM is responsible for improving team members' competencies.
  • Resources: PM must continually confirm resource availability and control physical resources (not just procurement).
