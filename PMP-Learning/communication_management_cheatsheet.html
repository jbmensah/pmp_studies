2. Communication Models

üß© How Messages Travel

Communication models help you visualise what‚Äôs happening when information moves between people. PMP focuses mainly on the basic sender‚Äìreceiver model and an interactive feedback-based model.

Basic Sender‚ÄìReceiver Model
  • Sender: Has an idea or message.
  • Encode: Turn idea into words, symbols, tone, slides, etc.
  • Channel: Email, voice, video, document, chat, etc.
  • Noise: Language barriers, distractions, poor audio, biases.
  • Receiver: Decodes through their own filters (experience, culture, assumptions).
  • No guarantee the message is understood as intended.
Interactive Model (With Feedback)
  • Receiver acknowledges the message (heard/read it).
  • Then sends feedback (questions, confirmation, different view).
  • Original sender now becomes the receiver and decodes the feedback.
  • Used well, this creates a feedback loop and shared understanding.
  • Great leaders explicitly ask: ‚ÄúIs this how you see it?‚Äù, ‚ÄúAnything I‚Äôve missed?‚Äù
üß≠ Exam hint: If the scenario shows information being broadcast with no feedback, that‚Äôs the basic sender‚Äìreceiver model. When you see check-back, acknowledgement, clarification, think interactive model.

3. Communication Skills & Methods

üó£Ô∏è Practical Skills

Communication isn‚Äôt only about tools; it‚Äôs about skills, behaviour, and channel choice.

Common Methods
  • Notice boards, press releases, annual reports.
  • Emails, letters to staff, chat tools.
  • Presentations & townhalls.
  • Phone calls, video calls.
  • Web portals, intranet, dashboards.
  • Focus groups, team meetings.
  • Social media & community channels.
Core Skills
  • Active listening: stay engaged, reflect back, ask clarifying questions.
  • Summarising: repeat key points to confirm understanding.
  • Cultural awareness: different preferences for eye contact, directness, formality.
  • Managing expectations: clarify what will be delivered and when.
  • Negotiation: align conflicting stakeholder expectations.
Feedback & Non-verbal
  • Feedback: reactions (verbal, written, non-verbal) to messages or deliverables.
  • Sources: 1:1 conversations, meetings, reports, surveys.
  • Non-verbal cues: posture, crossed arms, eye contact, mirroring, facial expression.
  • Tone of voice: bored vs curious vs enthusiastic can change the message.
Presentations
Purpose & Use
  • Share progress and status with stakeholders.
  • Provide background for decisions.
  • Explain objectives, risks, and options.
Good Practice
  • Design for your audience (execs vs team vs vendors).
  • Be clear on the decision or outcome you want.
  • Use visuals and summaries, not dense text.
üß≠ Exam hint: When the scenario shows repeated misunderstandings, the best next step often involves active listening, clarification, and feedback, not just sending another email.

üí° Clear Writing

The Five C‚Äôs help reduce misunderstandings in written communication. You‚Äôll see them referenced in PMP materials as a way to improve clarity.

Core Ideas

  • Concise expression ‚Äì remove unnecessary words; get to the point.

5. Push and Pull Communication

üß© Methods

PMP loves to test whether you understand the difference between push and pull communication methods.

Push Communication
  • Information is sent out to specific recipients.
  • Examples:
    • Emails, letters, memos, reports.
    • Voicemails, SMS, newsletters.
    • Press releases, announcements.
  • No guarantee it was received, read, or understood.
  • Often combined with follow-up or feedback to check understanding.
Pull Communication
  • Information is stored somewhere; stakeholders access it when they need it.
  • Examples:
    • Web portals, intranet sites, shared drives.
    • Dashboards and reports on demand.
    • Self-paced online learning.
    • Lessons learned repositories / knowledge bases.
  • Good when there is large volume or diverse stakeholder needs.
üß≠ Exam hint:
  • Need to ensureconfirmation / feedback.
  • Information is large, detailed, and for many people to access whenever? ‚Üí Use pull (portal, repository, dashboard).
